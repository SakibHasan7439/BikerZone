frequently asked questions

What are the most important things I should know about riding a bike?

Safety First! Always obey the rules of the road. Obey all traffic signals, signs, and laws. Get in the mindset of “driving” your bike—not just “riding” your bike. This will help you be a more focused and legally compliant bike rider.

How can I tell if my helmet is old and I need a new one?

Look inside the helmet for a label or sticker that indicates the manufacture date. Most helmets have a lifespan of about 5 years from the date of manufacture, even if they haven’t been involved in an accident.

My bike has been in storage is it safe to ride?

Tires can degrade over time, especially if they’ve been sitting in one position. Look for any signs of cracking or flat spots.Check the tread depth to make sure it’s adequate for safe riding.

What rules should I follow when riding my bike?

Always wear a helmet to protect your head in case of an accident.Wear long sleeves, pants, gloves, and shoes to protect your skin.
